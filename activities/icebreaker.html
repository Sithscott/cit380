Icebreaker

The icebreaker for this course will be a bit different than what you may have experienced in the past. While there will be an element of introduction, we are less concerned with where you are from, and more concerned with your ability to think and contribute meaningfully to a conversation.

Prepare

Begin your preparation for this activity by either choosing and studying a case study from the list below or by thinking about a project that you are already familiar with. Ponder the following questions as they relate to your chosen project.

Questions

  • How was the need for this project identified?
  • What was the purpose or mission of this project?
  • What was the scope of the creation project (did it have a start and finish - if so what were they)?
  • How was the entire effort organized?
  • What was the organizational structure like (who reported to whom)?
  • How was the project plan created, refined, discussed, decided upon, and communicated?
  • How was the progress or status of the project monitored and communicated?
  • How was the success or failure of project tasks evaluated or approved?
  • What were the phases or divisions of the project? (was there more than one layer?)
  • Were deadlines or time a factor?
  • Was there a budget or costs associated with the project?
  • Were there resources (people, tools, equipment, technology, materials, etc.) involved to achieve the project goals?

Post

Once you have formulated your thoughts post them in the icebreaker discussion board. Try to do this early in the week.

Reply

Later in the week read and reply to other posts. Focus on generating thoughtful responses. You are not evaluated on quantity here but on quality.

Review

Take a bit of time to read over the threads of the discussion. Read them with the following perspective: In weeks 2 and 3 you will be choosing a path for the course, proposing a project, and then either building a team for your proposal OR joining the team for someone else's project.

See if you can answer the following questions about your peers from their responses:

  • How well might I work with this person?
  • What roles in a project could you see this person filling well?
  • Are they thoughtful?
  • Did they actually contribute to the conversation?
  • Are they considerate?
  • How well did they complete this simple activity?
  • What do my posts tell my classmates about me?
