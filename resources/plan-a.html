Challenge Path details

Overview

The main focus of the Challenge path is to allow students to experiment deeply with the idea of "Acting and not being acted upon". You are given great flexibility with this path to determine everything about your project including:

  • Project management methodology
  • Deliverables
  • Milestones
  • Assessment method (how your project should be graded)

The assessment piece in particular should be negotiated and ultimately approved by the instructor. A challenge path project should be a group project.

There will be an expectation for your team to turn in a mid-project milestone report detailing your progress at the midpoint of your project, and a lessons learned report you will turn in at the end. The milestone report must be done no later than Week 6, the lessons learned report no later than week 12. Both of these can be completed much earlier if you are trying to finish the course early or are completing multiple projects.

These status reports should be submitted to the Project Milestone Reports discussion board in ILearn. Include any important project documentation or other artifacts that will be needed to understand the full status of your project along with your milestone report.

Additional information

A Project Management Plan (PMP) is a living document. Once you have completed yours make sure to regularly update it as necessary while you execute the project.

There will also be an expectation for you to review other student's projects. You can evaluate the projects either at the half-way point using the milestone mid-project report, or at the end using the lessons learned.

You can determine the format of this review, or you can look at common project post-mortem templates.

Start early on these reviews. Early feedback to the owners of the projects will be much more useful than late feedback. Likewise you should seek out early feedback on your project. Because of the way the course is structured there is no guarantee that your project will get reviewed by a peer. You can seek these out! From classmates, family, co-workers, instructors, peers, etc.

The projects to review will be found in the Project Milestone Reports discussion board in ILearn. Post your review or post-mortem as a reply in that same place.
