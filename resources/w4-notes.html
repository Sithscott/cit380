Project Management

CIT 380

Week 4 Teaching Notes

Outcomes

By the end of the semester, each student will:

  1. Be an effective communicator, team member, and leader
  2. Be proactive, able to operate in poorly defined or constrained situations
  3. Know how to create, coordinate, execute, and evaluate a project plan
  4. Be self-regulated, life-long learners

Before the Lesson Begins

  1. Plan to have an all-hands meeting (recorded synchronous video meeting) or prepare a video recording. This can also be done as an announcement some weeks. Alternate. There should be two parts:
    1. Summary of key take-aways from last week. Conversations you had with individual teams, themes you are noticing that multiple teams are struggling with, or another other advise related to the status of the overall class.
    2. Tales from the trenches: Tell a story that you feel will be helpful from your experience or from something you have witnessed. I would save these and recycle them for subsequent semesters as appropriate.
  2. Because not all students will have a reason to check into Ilearn regularly, you will either need to require it specifically, or prepare an email group and send important class communications out that way.
  3. Set up groups in I-Learn for each project if the project leader or the communication plan for a project needs it. Enroll the members of the projects to the respective groups. Project specific feedback, Questions, and such to the instructor go through this channel.

During the Lesson

  1. Grade the charters.
  2. Be as involved in the various student projects as possible. Many will not be in Ilearn often, but they should have specified how they as a team will be communicating with you and each other. Tune in to these conversations as often as you can. Your role for most of the next 9 weeks will be mentor and advisor. There will be a light grading load for the most part. As students finish up projects they should produce a lessons learned report detailing how the project went and schedule an appointment with you to close it out. Set your schedule in advance so they can find a time that works for both you and them. Students will also be required to review a certain number of projects throughout the semester. Grade those as they trickle in.
  3. Remind students as appropriate that projects and/or the course can be finished early. When you have the project close meeting focus on the habits they have developed, and the knowledge they have gained. Help them to understand that completion is not just ticking off all the boxes, but involves things like the student having developed the habit of continuous learning, and meeting the objectives of the course. Ultimately if they can show that they have met the course outcomes (or the outcomes from their assessment plan if they altered) they can be done.
  4. Inform students of your office hours.
  5. Answer student questions via email, office hours, and Announcements discussion.
