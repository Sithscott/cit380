Plan B details

Overview

Path B allows for students to still gain experience in becoming more proactive, but with more scaffolding to help them get started. They are given examples and ideas to help scaffold their project planning around. These projects can be completed as a group or individually.

If done individually a Plan B student can get a maximum of 85%. If done as a group the maximum grade is 95%

After settling on a project to complete you should then put some research and thought towards which Project management methodology would best fit with your project plans. Google is your friend here, or here is a sample list to get you started.

Methodology in hand, you should now prepare the Charter for your project and the project management plan. Your documentation list should include the following:

  4. Quality Management Plan:
  5. Risk Management plan:
  6. Communications Plan: (if done as a group)
  7. Scope Management Plan: (if done as a group)

This list is somewhat open for negotiation. You can omit or add to it provided you support your changes with good rationale.

All changes to the plan should be discussed and ultimately approved by the instructor.

Additional information

A Project Management Plan (PMP) is a living document. Once you have completed yours make sure to regularly update it as necessary while you execute the project.
